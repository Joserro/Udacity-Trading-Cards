Brown Bear

The winter hairs are thin, yet rough to the touch. The summer fur is much shorter and sparser, and its length and density varies geographically.

  • Scientific Name: Ursus arctos
  • Average Lenght: 8.3 to 14.2 in
  • Lifespan: 20 - 25 years.
  • Habitat: In Europe, the brown bear is mostly found in mountain woodlands, in Siberia it occurs primarily in forests while in North America they prefer tundra, alpine meadows and coastlines.

The Bear is a well-known animal. It is a big animal with either lighter or darker brown fur. Bears walk like humans do on their soles. It has dull teeth as all these who eat both plants and animals.
